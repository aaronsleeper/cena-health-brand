# Cena Health — Illustration System Guidelines

_Seed document for Brand Analyst and Visual Language Curator._
_Note: All color palette references, specific hex values, font names, and the "Gentle Strength" aesthetic label have been removed. Those are open design decisions for the agent pipeline. What remains are the principles, emotional architecture, subject treatment, and composition guidance -- which are considered strong and worth building from._

---

## Visual DNA

Four non-negotiable principles that define every illustration:

1. **Warm Clarity** -- Simple, legible forms that communicate immediately without visual noise. Every element serves understanding.
2. **Dignified Humanity** -- People are portrayed with respect and agency, never as victims or stereotypes. Bodies, abilities, and cultures are authentically diverse.
3. **Grounded Optimism** -- Honest about health challenges while conveying achievable progress. Avoids both clinical sterility and unrealistic cheerfulness.
4. **Thematic Cohesion** -- Every element within an illustration must feel as though the same illustrator created it. No text overlays, standalone icons, or graphic elements that break the illustrated aesthetic. Simplified elements are acceptable only when they serve the narrative and match the organic, hand-drawn quality of surrounding artwork.

## Emotional Architecture

### Primary: Gentle Empowerment

Patients feel capable and supported, not overwhelmed. Visual language says "you can do this with our help."

**Achieve through:**
- Forward-leaning body language, not passive or defeated poses
- People actively engaged (preparing food, using technology, connecting with others)
- Composition that places patients as central actors, not recipients
- Scale relationships that make technology/systems feel accessible, not imposing

**Avoid:**
- Heroic/triumphant poses (unrealistic)
- Passive receiving of care (disempowering)
- Overwhelmed or confused expressions
- Visual complexity that creates anxiety

### Secondary: Warm Trust

The feeling of a knowledgeable coach walking alongside, culturally competent and understanding.

**Achieve through:**
- Soft, organic line quality (hand-drawn feel, not geometric rigidity)
- Human-scaled environments (homes, community spaces vs. institutional settings)
- Visual connections between people (eye contact, shared activities, proximity)

**Avoid:**
- Corporate/institutional imagery
- Isolated individuals without context
- Authoritative/hierarchical spatial relationships

### Tertiary: Dignified Hope

Acknowledging challenges while conveying achievable progress without pity or false cheer.

**Achieve through:**
- Realistic scenarios with positive momentum (mid-journey, not just endpoints)
- Honest portrayal of effort alongside support systems
- Composition showing progress/direction without completion pressure

**Avoid:**
- Before/after transformation narratives
- Pitying or condescending visual metaphors
- Unrealistic perfection (pristine kitchens, flawless meals)
- Generic "wellness" aesthetics (yoga poses, green smoothies, meditation)

## Line & Form Language

### Line Quality

**Weight:** Variable line weight for warmth and hierarchy
- Primary elements: heavier stroke
- Secondary details: medium stroke
- Fine details/textures: lightest stroke
- Never uniform weight across entire illustration

**Style:** Organic with subtle imperfection
- Hand-drawn quality, not geometric precision
- Gentle curves over hard angles
- Slightly irregular edges (not perfectly smooth vectors)
- Confident, flowing strokes vs. tentative sketchy marks

**Caps & Joins:** Rounded
- Round line caps for softness
- Round joins at intersections
- Avoid sharp miters or hard corners

### Shape Philosophy

**Simple Illustrations:**
- Simplified geometric foundations (circles, rounded rectangles, organic blobs)
- 2-4 shapes maximum per icon
- Recognize at thumbnail size
- Avoid fine detail that disappears when scaled down

**Complex Scenes:**
- Build from simple shapes, add selective detail
- Foreground elements more detailed, background simplified
- Use negative space intentionally for breathing room
- Maximum detail density: 60% of composition (40% calm space)

### Element Integration

**Core Rule:** No text or icons within illustrations. All visual elements must share the same illustrative DNA.

**Prohibited Elements:**
- Text labels, captions, or typography overlaid on or embedded in illustrations
- Standalone icons or UI-style graphics mixed with illustrated content
- Geometric symbols that feel designed rather than drawn
- Infographic elements (arrows, badges, callout boxes) within narrative scenes

**Integration Test:** Before including any element, ask: "Does this look like the same artist drew it?" If an element feels imported from a different visual language, it does not belong.

### Texture & Pattern

**When to Use:**
- Add warmth and tactility to large flat areas
- Distinguish materials (fabric vs. paper vs. food)
- Create depth without color changes
- Cultural patterns for authenticity (textiles, ceramics)

**Avoid:**
- Heavy textures that reduce legibility
- Texture as default fill -- use intentionally

## Subject Library

### People

**Representation Principles:**
- Diverse ages, body types, skin tones, abilities, cultural backgrounds
- No tokenization -- characters should feel like individuals, not demographic checkboxes
- Authentic cultural details (clothing, hair, accessories, settings)
- Range of abilities shown naturally, not as inspirational narratives

**Body Language & Expression:**
- Active, engaged postures (preparing food, using phone, connecting with others)
- Neutral to gently positive expressions (avoid extreme emotions)
- Eye contact with other characters or viewer for connection
- Hands shown doing meaningful activities (not just posed)

**Family & Community Context:**
- Multi-generational groupings
- Caregiver relationships shown with dignity
- Shared meals and communal food preparation
- Technology use in family settings (not isolated individuals)

### Food

**Cultural Diversity:**
- Latin American cuisines: beans, rice, tortillas, platanos, mole, tamales
- Soul food traditions: greens, cornbread, sweet potatoes, okra
- Asian cuisines: rice, noodles, dumplings, stir-fries, curries
- Mediterranean foods: olive oil, whole grains, legumes, vegetables
- Avoid "clean eating" stereotypes (quinoa bowls, acai, etc.)

**Visual Treatment:**
- Recognizable, appetizing, realistic proportions
- Show food in context (plates, tables, hands preparing)
- Home cooking environments, not restaurant presentation

**Avoid:**
- Perfect plating or food styling
- Isolated food items without human context
- Green smoothies, salads, and stereotypical "diet" foods unless contextually relevant

### Technology & Platform

**Devices:**
- Smartphones (primary) -- accessible entry point, voice-first
- Tablets in clinical/community settings
- Laptops for care coordination

**Human-Technology Relationship:**
- Technology as tool in hands, not dominant focus
- Eye contact with people, not screens, when possible
- Devices shown in natural home/community environments
- Multi-generational technology use (not age-segregated)

### Healthcare Elements

**Avoid:**
- Stethoscopes, lab coats (overly clinical)
- Hospital beds, IV drips (anxiety-inducing)
- Complex medical equipment (intimidating)
- White, sterile environments

**Show Instead:**
- Community health centers
- Home health visits
- Telehealth on family devices
- Simple health tracking (not medicalized)

## Composition Patterns

### Simple Illustrations (Icons & Spots)

**Layout Systems:**
- **Centered/Symmetrical** -- Stability, trust (medical concepts, platform features)
- **Off-center/Balanced** -- Approachable, human (food, people, activities)
- **Diagonal Energy** -- Progress, action (outcomes, growth, connection)

**Negative Space Rules:**
- Minimum 30% breathing room around primary subject
- Test legibility at small sizes

**Focal Points:**
- Single clear focal point
- Supporting elements maximum 40% visual weight of primary
- Avoid competing elements of equal visual weight

### Complex Narrative Scenes

**Layered Depth:**
- Foreground (40%): Primary characters and key actions, highest detail
- Midground (30%): Supporting characters or contextual elements
- Background (30%): Environmental context, minimal detail

**Narrative Flow:**
- Left-to-right progression for time/process
- Eye path through color contrast and detail density
- Use overlapping elements to create connections

**Scale Relationships:**
- People at realistic proportions to environment (not dwarfed by systems)
- Technology appropriately sized (accessible, not imposing)
- Buildings/institutions suggested, not dominating

## Application Contexts

### Investor Pitch Decks
- Clean, professional execution
- Complex scenes showing care coordination workflows
- Icons for outcome metrics
- Support data without competing with it
- Horizontal orientation (16:9)

### Patient-Facing Materials
- Maximum warmth and approachability
- Simple, clear communication (no jargon)
- Culturally specific representation
- Bilingual considerations (visual-first, text-second)
- Vertical orientation for mobile/print
- Generous negative space

### Platform/Product UI
- Maximum simplicity and clarity
- Consistent icon system
- Immediate recognition at small sizes
- Functional beauty (not decorative)

### Clinical Documentation
- Professional, evidence-based aesthetic
- Support data and text, don't dominate
- Simple diagrams and process flows
- Conservative execution (trust-building)

## Do's and Don'ts

### DO
- Show patients actively engaged in their health journey
- Portray realistic scenarios with achievable progress
- Use warm, organic visual language that feels human
- Represent cultural diversity authentically and specifically
- Show technology as accessible tool, not barrier or cure-all
- Include family and community contexts for health decisions
- Use variable line weight for hierarchy and warmth
- Create intentional negative space (30%+ of composition)

### DON'T
- Show patients as passive recipients of care
- Use before/after transformation narratives
- Portray pity, sympathy, or condescension
- Rely on stereotypical "wellness" aesthetics
- Isolate individuals without community context
- Use uniform line weight throughout
- Add text labels or typography within illustrations
- Mix icons or UI-style elements with illustrated content
- Default to generic "healthy" food stereotypes
- Show intimidating medical equipment

## Quality Control Checklist

- [ ] Variable line weight applied
- [ ] Rounded caps and joins throughout
- [ ] Minimum 30% negative space
- [ ] Legible at target size
- [ ] No text or typography embedded in illustration
- [ ] No icons or UI elements mixed with illustrated content
- [ ] All elements feel thematically unified (same illustrator test)
- [ ] WCAG AA contrast ratios met (4.5:1 minimum)
- [ ] Meaning not conveyed by color alone
- [ ] Shows agency and empowerment (not passivity)
- [ ] Realistic scenarios (not before/after transformations)
- [ ] Human connection visible (not isolated individuals)
- [ ] Context-appropriate (investor/patient/clinical/marketing)
